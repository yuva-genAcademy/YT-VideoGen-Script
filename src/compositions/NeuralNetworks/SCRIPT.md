# Neural Networks — 60-Second Script

**Target:** 160 wpm | ~150 words | ~56s narration + 4s visual holds

---

## [TITLE] 0–6 s

Neural networks are the engine powering modern AI.

---

## [NEURON] 6–21 s *(15 s | ~40 words)*

Inspired by the human brain, they're built from layers of nodes called neurons.
Each neuron takes inputs, multiplies them by weights — which determine importance —
sums everything up, and passes the result through an activation function.
That function decides whether the neuron fires.

---

## [LAYERS] 21–41 s *(20 s | ~53 words)*

Neurons are organized into layers.
The input layer receives raw data — pixels, text tokens, numbers.
Hidden layers extract increasingly complex patterns.
The output layer delivers the final prediction.
Stack more hidden layers and you get deep learning —
networks capable of recognizing faces, translating languages, and generating text.

---

## [TRAINING] 41–53 s *(12 s | ~33 words)*

Networks learn through backpropagation.
The model makes a prediction, measures the error, then propagates it backwards —
nudging every weight to reduce the loss.
Repeat millions of times, and the network learns.

---

## [OUTRO] 53–60 s *(7 s | ~17 words)*

Neural networks now power image recognition, language models, and recommendations.
They're just math — applied at scale.
