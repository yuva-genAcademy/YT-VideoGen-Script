Practical Implications
Backprop Failure Modes
Problem 01
Differentiable Ops
Required
Every operation must have a gradient. Hard step functions cannot be backpropped through.
ReLU smooth, differentiable activation
Problem 02
Vanishing
Gradients
Gradients shrink to near-zero as they propagate back through many layers. Early weights cannot learn.
Residual Connections skip connections preserve gradient flow
Problem 03
Exploding
Gradients
The inverse: gradients grow unbounded, destabilizing weight updates entirely.
BatchNorm normalizes activations per layer
These are not cosmetic choices. They are structural responses to backprop failure modes.
